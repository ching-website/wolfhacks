About Us

Chinguacousy Secondary School is proud to present it's and Peel's very first hackathon, WolfHacks. WolfHacks will bring together more than 100 bright and talented students who will design and demonstrate their solutions to persistent problems. Students will be taking part in many fun and interactive workshops as well as get opportunities to talk to mentors and other industry figures.

FAQ

What is WolfHacks?

WolfHacks is a 12 hour hackathon that allows students from diverse backgrounds to come together and find innovative solutions to specific problems.

How much does it cost?

Absolutely nothing! There is no entry fee. Breakfast, lunch, and dinner will be provided free of cost, while snacks will be available throughout the day if you get hungry. Unfortunately, we are not able to reimburse travel costs.

Where is the event?

WolfHacks will be hosted at Chinguacousy Secondary School, 1370 Williams Parkway, Brampton, ON.

What if I don't know how to code?

Hackathons are beginner friendly. Attending a hackathon is an excellent way to learn how to code and strengthen existing coding skills. We'll have experienced mentors who can help groups who may not have attended a hackathon before, or are new to programming in general.

Will there be food?

Yes! Meals and snacks will be free and accessible to you throughout the day. If you have any dietary restrictions be sure to specify them in your application.

What is Major League Hacking?

Major League Hacking is the official student hackathon league in North America & Europe. We work with over 200 Member Events and empower over 70,000 students every year.

What should I bring?

Your laptop, phone, chargers, student ID, and extension cords.

What if I don't have a team?

Check for existing teams in local circles, like your local makerspace or your school's computer science club. You can even assemble your own team with your friends. Remember, you or your teammates don't necessarily require programming knowledge, so feel free to bring anyone who's interested. Teams, however, are restricted to four members.

Is there a code of conduct?

Yes, there is. We will be enforcing it in full with no exceptions. You can find it here.
